# Day 92 brief — Pentagon trilateral convenes with substance, Brent collapses to $92.56 on 60-day MOU framework, Lebanon MOH records single deadliest day of the cycle

*Day 92 of Operation Epic Fury — 2026-05-30*

## Executive Summary

Day 92 splits the war's two tracks more cleanly than any prior day of the deal cycle. The Pentagon trilateral US-Israel-Lebanon military meeting convened Friday May 29 with substance: six LAF officers under Brigadier General Olivier Hekmat met six IDF officers under US defense-ministry supervision, and the Lebanese Army presented a phased plan to consolidate Hezbollah weapons under state authority (US Department of State, Haaretz, Fox News). Brent crude collapsed to $92.56 — down 1.2% on the day, ~19% on the month for its worst monthly print since Covid — as markets priced the tentative 60-day MOU framework: 60-day extension, "unrestricted" Hormuz, 30 days for Iran to remove mines, an Iranian no-nuclear-weapon commitment, top-priority 60-day removal of Iran's enriched uranium, $24 billion in frozen assets potentially released (CNBC, Trading Economics, Al Jazeera, Euronews, Jerusalem Post). Cutting the other way, Lebanon Health Ministry recorded the single deadliest day of the cycle (55 KIA / 187 WIA, war-cumulative 3,324 / 10,027) and the IDF announced Golani Recon completed a weeklong raid at Zawtar al-Sharqiyah ~10 km past the Litani — deepest truce-period ground penetration (Pravda EN, PBS NewsHour, Times of Israel). Tasnim: text "not yet finalized"; neither Mojtaba Khamenei nor Trump has signed off (CNN). Analytical judgment: 30-day ceasefire probability revises up from 19 to 23; direction holds escalating; seven-day risk holds conditional with Round 4 political June 2-3.

## Strategic Implications

**1. The Pentagon trilateral convened with substance — six-to-six military officers, a phased LAF Hezbollah-disarmament plan tabled — and the State Department's framing splitting the Lebanese state from Hezbollah is the most consequential US public posture shift of the truce period.**

Six LAF officers under Brigadier General Olivier Hekmat, Lebanon's military attaché in Washington, met six IDF officers under US defense-ministry supervision (Fox News, The Media Line, The National). The Lebanese Army presented a phased plan to consolidate Hezbollah's weapons under state authority south of the Litani in phase one and reaffirmed readiness to deploy across the south to make it a weapons-free zone (Haaretz, Kataeb). The State Department readout described "Hizballah's continued attacks on Israel, without the consent or approval of the Government of Lebanon, undertaken in order to derail this process" — a US public framing splitting the Lebanese state from Hezbollah for the first time in the truce period and giving Beirut cover to enforce the LAF phased plan. Analytical judgment: under the multi-clock framework the active-deadline clock improved on a meeting whose pre-convening kinetic posture had argued against a breakthrough; the LAF phased plan converts the disarmament agenda from rhetorical contest into a tabled document. Skeptical counter: the LAF plan was pre-staged in Beirut messaging since mid-May, and Pentagon tabling is not Hezbollah accepting it; Qassem's "hell for Israel" line stands; the deepest Israeli truce-period ground penetration was announced the same day.

**2. Brent's collapse to $92.56 — worst monthly print since Covid — and the publicly-named 60-day MOU terms hardened the deal architecture into specific obligations even as Tehran's political clearance lapsed past the Pentagon convening.**

Brent fell to $92.56 Friday, down 1.2% on the day and ~19% on the month (CNBC, Trading Economics). The MOU framework concretized: a 60-day truce extension; "unrestricted" Hormuz shipping with no tolls and no "harassment"; 30 days for Iran to remove all mines; an Iranian commitment not to develop a nuclear weapon; top-priority 60-day removal of Iran's enriched uranium; $24 billion in frozen Iranian assets potentially released, half potentially on announcement (Al Jazeera, Euronews, Jerusalem Post, CNN). Per CNN sources Mojtaba Khamenei endorsed the broad template but had not approved the drafted MOU; Trump also had not agreed. Tasnim continued to frame the text as "not yet finalized." Analytical judgment: publicly-named terms move the deal from the Day 90-91 rhetorical gap into a posture where both sides are negotiating specific clauses. Under the multi-clock framework the negotiation-capacity clock is improving on terms-naming, and the energy-infrastructure clock improves on Brent's retracement below CPC's $110 floor. Skeptical counter: terms-naming is not signature, and the UAE-Iran disclosure (Implication 3) reminds publicly that the Gulf-Israeli kinetic record on Iran is deeper than Tehran has publicly absorbed. Another week without movement converts the political-clearance pause into stall.

**3. Taiwan: Brent collapses below CPC's $110 working floor for the first time in the cycle — but the WSJ disclosure of UAE coordinated strikes on Iranian oil and petrochemical assets sharpens, not eases, the structural Hormuz tail.**

Brent's $92.56 print is the first sub-$110 print of the deal cycle and pulls below Taiwan CPC's $110 working floor for the first time — opening ~$17 of margin against contract-linked LNG (CNBC, Trading Economics). For Taiwan the immediate read is favorable: cover through September is confirmed, and the Brent retracement to a sub-CPC handle is the strongest market signal of the cycle that the 60-day MOU framework is in the price. The structural read is less favorable. The WSJ disclosure (via Times of Israel and Haaretz) that the UAE conducted dozens of airstrikes on Iran during the war and post-truce — coordinated with Israel and the US, including Qush, Abu Musa, Bandar Abbas, Lavan Island's oil refinery, and Asaluyeh petrochemical — surfaces a previously-suppressed regional kinetic axis Tehran is absorbing in the same week MOU political clearance was running through Mojtaba Khamenei's office. China still takes ~37.7% of Hormuz crude flows; Wang Yi's "resistance and steadfastness" line carries. Analytical judgment: the energy-infrastructure clock improved on Brent's collapse; the structural Hormuz tail sharpened on the UAE-Israel-US disclosure. TSMC's 2026 CapEx framework-signature path holds near 8%. No fresh Taiwan-specific development today.
