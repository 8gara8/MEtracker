## Strategic Implications

**1. The Cooper-Caine kinetic briefing reframes the Wednesday "extended blockade" from single-instrument to two-instrument squeeze.**

Wednesday Trump told Axios the blockade was the leverage; Thursday Trump received a CENTCOM briefing on a "short and powerful" wave of strikes, a Hormuz seizure option using US ground forces to reopen the strait by force, and a special-forces option to secure Iran's HEU stockpile. The two postures are not contradictory — they are the bracketing of Iran's response space. The blockade is the patient instrument; the strike package is the impatient one. Trump's Wednesday "more effective than the bombing" framing means the kinetic option is reserved as the consequence of Iranian non-capitulation rather than the next move, but the briefing changes Tehran's calculation: Iranian intransigence under blockade now risks both regime-cohesion damage from the rial collapse AND infrastructure damage from a documented strike option. The Hormuz seizure variant is the one that worries the channel most — it converts Iranian Hormuz command-and-control from negotiating chip into immediate liability. Pakistan's Friday revised proposal must now embed both nuclear language Trump will accept and Hormuz language that pre-empts the seizure option. Analytical judgment: probability moves 25 → 22; Pakistan's land-corridor opening (Implication 2) is the structural offset that prevents collapse to ~18.

**2. Pakistan's six overland routes converts Islamabad from pure mediator to mediator-plus-trade-partner — and structurally fragments the blockade architecture.**

The Transit of Goods through Territory of Pakistan Order is the first material breach of the Hormuz blockade architecture from a non-Iranian, non-Russia/China actor. Six overland routes from Karachi (where thousands of containers are stranded by the maritime blockade) into Iran via Quetta and Zahedan corridors materially relieves the rial-cohesion pressure track that was Wednesday's bilateral-squeeze theory. Tehran's economic clock now extends; the financial-collapse track does not break the regime in the four-mediator-channel timeframe Trump's blockade calculus assumed. The trade-off for Pakistan: Islamabad now has commercial skin in Iranian survival, complicating the pure-mediator role; the Organiser's "Did Pakistan betray Trump?" framing is the US-side allied-cohesion strain. Analytical judgment: the corridor opening reframes the Pakistan mediator role as Pakistan-plus-economic-architecture and reduces the Pakistan-Iran channel-collapse cost; Friday's revised proposal can now embed harder Iranian terms because Tehran's economic survival horizon has lengthened past Trump's blockade timeline. The Brent print at $126 intraday and the rial at 1.81M/USD now operate inside this offset rather than as a single bilateral compounding squeeze.

**3. Taiwan / CPC procurement: the Brent $126 wartime intraday is the kinetic-tail-risk boundary now priced into the curve.**

For Taiwan and CPC, the Thursday Brent intraday $126.41 is the kinetic-tail-risk boundary the back-month had been hedging since Wednesday's $118 close — and the CENTCOM briefing on a Hormuz-seizure variant is the upper-bound that print represents. The Citi $150-by-end-June scenario (Wednesday's operating curve) now sits inside a wider distribution: structural floor at the $115 OPEC-fragmentation-plus-extended-blockade level, central case Citi $150, kinetic-tail $130–$170 on the strikes-plus-Hormuz-seizure variant. CPC procurement should treat the Pakistan land corridor (Implication 2) as a downside relief signal — it lengthens the conflict's financial-track horizon, delays Iranian forced concession, and therefore extends the curve's structural-supply-damage timeline. MOEA's no-Hormuz-through-October scenario remains central case; CPC coal-rotation through summer holds with residual-LNG-procurement floor moving up. TSMC 2026 CapEx pricing pass-through now operates above $115 floor with kinetic-tail $170 as procurement-stress-test ceiling. The asymmetric trilateral (Russia overt; China overt via Wang Yi's "might over right" carry; Iran sanctioned-channel supply now augmented by Pakistan land-corridor optionality) reduces the supply-volatility-tail Beijing was managing. Analytical judgment: for the four-clock Taiwan frame the dominant procurement signal is duration — even a Day 70 ceasefire would not restore prior cost structure; absent ceasefire the kinetic option pushes near-term volatility floor higher.
