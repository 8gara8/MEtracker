## Current war status

Day 138 is the first genuinely two-sided day in a week: the blockade became a fact and the toll died. From 20:00 GMT the US Navy began enforcing its blockade of Iran's ports and coastline, and within hours it was biting — warships turned back ~21 vessels in the Arabian Sea, intercepted and reversed a US-sanctioned Chinese tanker, and ordered two tankers out of Chabahar to turn around, though at least one other Chinese hull still transited Hormuz (cordon real but porous) (Newsweek, Army Recognition, The Hill). Carve-outs narrow it below Monday's 'seal everything' framing: transit to non-Iranian destinations is not impeded and humanitarian cargo is permitted subject to inspection (NPR, CNN). But the kinetic line still rose: CENTCOM struck Iran a fourth straight night, and the IRGC answered with a fresh salvo on Sheikh Isa air base (Bahrain) and Ali al-Salem (Kuwait), reported as damaging drones and depots (Newsweek, Press TV). Brent settled $84.73 (+1.72%) after touching $87 intraday — first $87 print since June, week +10% — the toll climbdown capping the spike (CNBC, OilPrice). India summoned Iran's deputy chief of mission over the UAE tanker strikes; Araghchi said Iran is working Oman, Qatar and Pakistan to de-escalate even as deputies vowed Washington would 'regret' its strikes. 30-day ceasefire probability holds at 12. Direction stays escalating; 7-day risk extreme; spillover critical.
