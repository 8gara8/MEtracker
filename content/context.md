## Current war status

Day 143 moved the war into the nuclear file. Iran's Atomic Energy Organization said US projectiles struck the partly built Darkhovin nuclear power plant in Khuzestan early Sunday; the IAEA opened an inquiry, confirmed the site is in very early construction and held no nuclear material at its last visit, said no radiological risk is likely, and Grossi repeated his call for military restraint near all nuclear-related sites (AEOI, IAEA, UPI, Iran International). Near-zero radiological consequence is what makes it legible as signalling: the nuclear target category is now open. The American toll rose a third time in four days — CENTCOM said a service member was killed in action in northern Iraq on July 18 during a controlled detonation of unexploded ordnance from a downed Iranian one-way attack drone, with a second wounded, and on Sunday recovered unidentified human remains at the Muwaffaq Salti site in Jordan, under examination against the MIA (CENTCOM, Defense News, Axios, RFE/RL). Tehran answered legally: Deputy FM Kazem Gharibabadi announced Iran is suspending its June 17 MoU commitments over repeated US violations; Trump said he 'couldn't care less' (Press TV, CNN). Mojtaba Khamenei — unheard from publicly since March — called for 'sacred unity,' amplified within hours by Ghalibaf and by wartime joint command chief Ali Abdollahi, after ultra-hardliners accused the leadership of plotting a coup; Araghchi kept backing a negotiated end. US strikes ran an eighth consecutive night Saturday, framed by CENTCOM as punishment for Jordan, with a ninth wave Sunday. Iran fired on Kuwait and Bahrain, igniting a Kuwaiti power and water plant for a third straight day; the IRGC claimed it halted vessels in Hormuz while CENTCOM said its blockade redirected six ships and disabled one. Brent reached $88.10. Direction escalating; 7-day risk extreme; spillover critical. 30-day ceasefire probability falls to 4.

## Diplomatic state

Day 143 stripped the last procedural cover from both sides. Iran suspended its MoU commitments rather than abrogating them, a deliberately reversible act by a foreign ministry preserving an instrument the supreme leader had already called 'worthless and invalid' two days earlier — and Washington declined to treat the distinction as meaningful, Trump saying he 'couldn't care less.' Neither party now claims to be inside a framework. Inside Iran the story is cohesion, not diplomacy: Mojtaba Khamenei's first public message since March commanded 'sacred unity,' echoed within hours by Ghalibaf and by the head of the wartime joint command, in direct response to ultra-hardliner accusations that Ghalibaf and Pezeshkian were plotting a coup. A leadership that must publicly command unity is disclosing that it lacks it — and that same gap is why Araghchi could spend Sunday backing a negotiated end while the IRGC bombarded the Gulf. Great-power register unchanged: Beijing condemns the port blockade, Moscow warns that strikes push Iran toward weaponization rather than away.
