## Current war status

Day 92 splits the war's two tracks more cleanly than any prior day of the deal cycle. The Pentagon trilateral US-Israel-Lebanon military meeting convened Friday May 29 with substance: six LAF officers under Brigadier General Olivier Hekmat, Lebanon's military attaché in Washington, met six IDF officers under US defense-ministry supervision; the Lebanese Army presented a phased plan to consolidate Hezbollah weapons under state authority south of the Litani in phase one and reaffirmed readiness to deploy across the south to make it a weapons-free zone; the published five-item agenda was taken up; political-track talks set for Saturday May 30 "provided military talks succeed" (US Department of State, Haaretz, Fox News, The Media Line, The National, Kataeb). The State Department readout described Hezbollah's "continued attacks on Israel, without the consent or approval of the Government of Lebanon, undertaken in order to derail this process" — the first US public framing of the truce period to split the Lebanese state from Hezbollah. Brent crude collapsed to $92.56 Friday — down 1.2% on the day, ~19% on the month for the worst monthly print since Covid — as markets priced the tentative 60-day MOU framework: 60-day extension, "unrestricted" Hormuz shipping with no tolls and no "harassment," 30 days for Iran to remove all mines, an Iranian commitment not to develop a nuclear weapon, top-priority 60-day removal of Iran's enriched uranium, $24 billion in frozen assets potentially released with half potentially on signing (CNBC, Trading Economics, Al Jazeera, Euronews, Jerusalem Post). The Wall Street Journal disclosed (via Times of Israel and Haaretz) that the UAE conducted dozens of strikes on Iran during the war and after the truce in coordination with Israel and the US — Qush and Abu Musa islands, Bandar Abbas, Lavan Island's oil refinery, and the Asaluyeh petrochemical complex among the targets, with damage publicly attributed only to "fire" prior to the disclosure. Cutting the other way, Lebanon's Health Ministry recorded the single deadliest day of the cycle: 55 killed and 187 injured across southern and eastern Lebanon over 24 hours, war-cumulative civilians 3,324 / 10,027; the IDF announced the Golani Brigade Reconnaissance Unit had completed a weeklong raid at Zawtar al-Sharqiyah ~10 km past the Litani — the deepest publicly-acknowledged truce-period ground penetration. Tasnim continued to frame the agreement text as "not yet finalized or made definitive"; per CNN sources Mojtaba Khamenei endorsed the broad template of the 60-day MOU but had not approved the drafted text, and Trump also had not signed off. The 30-day ceasefire probability revises up from 19 to 23; direction holds escalating on the Lebanon kinetic high; seven-day risk holds conditional with Round 4 political talks Washington June 2-3.
