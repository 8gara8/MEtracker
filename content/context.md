## Current war status

Day 147 crossed the line the market and the mediators had watched all week: the Houthi embargo moved from reroute to interdiction. The diplomatic track deepened without advancing: the Oman-brokered, Iran-authored proposal now specifies a "middle corridor" through Hormuz between Omani and Iranian waters, reopening both lanes, carried to Washington by Qatari, Egyptian, Omani and Pakistani officials — but the US wants a longer ceasefire and navigation guarantees first, and neither Trump nor Iran has accepted (Jerusalem Post, Axios, Press TV). Per §3.5.3 the proposal is still an unaccepted claim. US toll holds at 17. Direction escalating; 7-day risk extreme; spillover critical; 30-day ceasefire probability holds at 10.

## Diplomatic state

Day 147 advanced both tracks and the escalatory one moved further. The Oman-brokered architecture is the most concrete it has been — broker, venue, author, multi-state carriage (Qatar/Egypt/Oman/Pakistan) and now a mapped "middle corridor" through Hormuz — yet acceptance is absent, the US wants a longer ceasefire and navigation guarantees first, and the tanker attack sits outside the proposal's scope. Against that, Trump's move to hold Iran responsible for Houthi strikes and to weigh a "massive attack," backed by a twelfth consecutive strike night and massing forces, is the louder signal. Per §3.5.3 this remains process over agreement: the venue and blueprint are real, the signature is not, and the gap between "talks continue" and "ships move safely" is the same gap that collapsed the April 8 and June frameworks.
