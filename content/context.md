## Current war status

Day 108 is the morning after the war's biggest de-escalation since the April 8 ceasefire — and the morning Israel's Beirut strike put it at risk. Late on Day 107, President Trump declared the deal "now complete," authorized an end to the US naval blockade ("Ships of the World, start your engines. Let the oil flow!"), and Pakistan announced the US and Iran had reached an agreement to end the war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz, with both sides declaring "the immediate and permanent termination of military operations on all fronts, including in Lebanon" (PBS/AP, RFE/RL). The blockade-end order is the first concrete US drawdown action of the war. Per §3.5.3 it is a mediator's declaration, not a landed signature: full terms were unreleased, Iran signaled no final decision, and the formal signing is set for Friday, June 19, in Switzerland (Times of Israel). The nuclear core is deferred to a 60-day post-signing track, not resolved — ~440.9 kg of 60% HEU stays in Iranian hands, now sealed in mined tunnels per CNN. On announcement day the Lebanon front detonated: Israel struck a Hezbollah command center in Beirut's Dahiyeh suburb (commander killed, four wounded) after Hezbollah drones hit northern Israel; Iran's Supreme National Security Council called its response "imminent" and Israel braced for missile fire; Trump faulted the strike ("should not have happened"). 30-day ceasefire probability jumps to 50 from 33; direction turns de-escalating; 7-day risk conditional, bimodal on the Friday signature and the Beirut flashpoint.
