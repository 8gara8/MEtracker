## Current war status

Day 123 turns de-escalating for the first time since the Day 121 base strikes. The mutual US–Iran kinetic standdown agreed late on Day 122 — "We decided to stop all the kinetic activity," a senior US official said — held into Day 123, and the two governments' technical delegations were set to meet in Doha, Qatar today, a session pulled from a postponed Geneva nuclear round and refocused on management of shipping in the Strait of Hormuz and de-escalation, with Qatar and Pakistan mediating (Times of Israel, Euronews, Axios, NPR). The base-on-base exchange that defined the war's most dangerous mode paused: no fifth US strike round, no new Iranian strikes on Gulf bases; vessels moved and Brent sat near $72.68 for August — the lowest since February 27 — with Energy Secretary Wright citing ~20 million barrels exiting Hormuz in 24 hours (Trading Economics, RFE/RL). Skeptical counter per §3.5.3: the de-escalation rests on a contested premise. Trump said Iran "requested" the meeting and asked for the halt; Iran's Tasnim called that "an absolute lie," Deputy FM Gharibabadi said the Doha talks were "not confirmed" until conditions are met, and FM Araghchi insisted the Strait stays under Iran's "total oversight and management" for 30 days (CBS News, Al Jazeera, NPR). Lebanon cut the other way: Israel destroyed a 200-metre Hezbollah tunnel network (hundreds of weapons, dozens of drones, four launch shafts) and kept striking Nabatieh (Lebanese Health Ministry: two wounded by a stun grenade) as Naim Qassem held the Washington framework "null and void" and a "surrender of sovereignty" amid mass Beirut protests, with Israel reported "bracing for attack" (Times of Israel, The Cradle, Al Jazeera). The nuclear sequencing dispute widened: Grossi says interim-deal inspections are "going to happen," Iran says only after a final agreement, and hardliners began demanding nuclear deterrence as a non-negotiable precondition (NPR, ISIS). 30-day ceasefire probability rises to 57 from 52; direction is de-escalating because both sides stopped the base-on-base exchange and returned to the table — conditionally, because the halt is under 48 hours old, the talks' very existence is disputed, and Lebanon is escalating in parallel.
