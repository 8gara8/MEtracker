## Current war status

Day 120 turns de-escalating — two of the week's hardest signals broke positive against a still-contested maritime picture. In Washington on Friday, Israel and Lebanon signed a US-brokered framework agreement, the first since the war began; ambassadors Leiter and Hamadeh signed at the State Department as Secretary Rubio called it a "first step" toward "lasting peace and security" (CNBC, Axios, Al Jazeera, NBC News, Jerusalem Post). The deal ties a ceasefire to Hezbollah's full cessation and evacuation from the South Litani Sector, advances pilot zones for exclusive Lebanese Armed Forces control, creates a US-facilitated trilateral Military Coordination Group, and pledges $100M in US humanitarian aid plus LAF training — but it is a framework, not a withdrawal: Israel "returns to its borders" only once the threat is "removed," Netanyahu vowed to hold the south-Lebanon buffer zone "until Hezbollah disarms," and Hezbollah accused Israel of a "flagrant" breach the same day, with Lebanon's health ministry confirming three civilians killed in a drone strike on a car in Mayfadoun (Times of Israel). The second signal is the market's: oil round-tripped to pre-conflict levels despite Thursday's vessel strike — Brent settled −4.34% to $71.99, a ~10% weekly loss back to pre-war levels, WTI below $70 — as more tankers exited Hormuz (CNBC, OilPrice). Holding seven-day risk conditional: the IRGC route-control warning stands, the IMO evacuation pause holds, transits run a fraction of the 130–140/day norm, and the nuclear-inspection sequencing dispute is unresolved. 30-day ceasefire probability ticks up to 73; direction is de-escalating because a signed Israel–Lebanon framework and oil at pre-war lows are the hardest evidence the week produced — even hedged.
