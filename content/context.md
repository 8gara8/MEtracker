## Current war status

Day 132 turned the maritime break into a two-way kinetic war. After US officials attributed the three July 7 Hormuz vessel strikes to the IRGC (Al Rekayyat, Wedyan, Cyprus Prosperity), US Central Command struck more than 80 targets across Iran — air defenses, command-and-control, coastal radar, anti-ship missile sites, and 60+ IRGC small boats — the first US kinetic strikes on Iranian soil since the June ceasefire; Sentinel-2 imagery confirmed heavy damage to an IRGC Navy base in southern Iran (Jerusalem Post, Times of Israel, Liveuamap). Iran answered within hours: the IRGC said it struck 85 US military targets in Bahrain (Fifth Fleet base area at Port Salman) and Kuwait's Ali Salem Air Base and downed an MQ-9 drone, warning of a "harsher" retaliation in the coming days (RFE/RL, PBS, NBC). At the NATO summit in Ankara, Trump declared the MoU "over" and the talks a "waste of time" — while saying he "might" let them continue — and threatened a second strike day, reimposition of the naval blockade, and hits on Iran's electrical grid (Al Jazeera, CNBC, CNN). Brent jumped ~5.2% to settle at $78.02, WTI +4.4% to $73.52 (CNBC). Khamenei's six-day funeral reached its final rite — burial at Mashhad's Imam Reza shrine July 9. Neither side formally withdrew from the June 17 MoU; the post-burial Doha resumption stayed technically on the calendar but badly damaged. 30-day ceasefire probability falls to 25 (−27 from 52). Direction stays escalating, now on a two-way kinetic axis.
