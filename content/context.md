## Current war status

Day 131 broke the standdown. The Iran-US kinetic pause that had held for roughly ten days ended at sea: US officials say the IRGC struck three commercial vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz between Monday night and Tuesday morning — the most in any single day since the June ceasefire — including the Qatari LNG carrier Al Rekayyat (Nakilat, laden from Ras Laffan), hit by a projectile ~8nm east of Limah, Oman and set afire, and a Saudi-flagged crude tanker damaged in the same window (CNN, Bloomberg, Al Jazeera). It is the first LNG hull struck since the war began; no casualties or spill reported. Iran did not claim the attacks — state TV said a vessel was hit only after ignoring warnings, and neither CENTCOM nor the IRGC formally commented (Business Standard, Al Jazeera) — reading as calibrated deniability, not full rupture (§3.5.6 direct-confrontation prior). Washington answered within hours by revoking the OFAC general license (General License X) that had authorized Iranian oil sales through Aug 21, calling the MoU "entirely performance-based," with a ten-day wind-down into blocked, interest-bearing US accounts (NOTUS, CNBC, Axios). Energy repriced per §3.5.5: Brent settled +3% at $74.16 (one-week high, up from below $72) and European gas rose as much as 4.5%; the strait's southern corridor stayed open (≥8 Japan-linked ships, incl. 5 supertankers, still exited via a route near Iran) (CNBC, Trading Economics, gCaptain). Against the down-leg, the diplomatic table survived: Qatar's FM spokesman al-Ansari called the Doha indirect talks "positive progress" and Ghalibaf called implementation "difficult, but achievable," with the Mashhad burial set for July 9 ahead of a targeted July 11 resumption (CNN, Al Jazeera). 30-day ceasefire probability falls to 52 (−10). Direction turns escalating.
