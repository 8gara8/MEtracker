## Current war status

Day 144 moved in two directions at once for the first time since the MoU suspension. Yemen's Houthis declared a maritime embargo on Saudi Arabia effective immediately — Yahya Saree calling it 'an eye for an eye' for the ten-year Saudi siege of Yemen and the bombing of Sanaa airport, deputy media chief Nasruddin Amer saying Bab el-Mandeb will be closed (Al Jazeera, Euronews, UPI, NBC, CNBC). US forces struck a tenth consecutive night from 4pm ET Monday — Bandar Abbas, Shiraz, Sirik, Bandar Lengeh, Qeshm, Khomein — with CENTCOM reframing the rationale from punishment for Jordan back to degrading Iran's ability to attack Hormuz shipping. The nuclear question was bounded, not answered: air defenses engaged at Bushehr but a provincial official reported no plant damage and IRNA quoted Isfahan's governor denying any strike. Brent fell to ~$82 from $88. Direction mixed; 7-day risk extreme; spillover critical. 30-day ceasefire probability rises to 8.

## Diplomatic state

Day 144 restarted a clock that had run to zero. Iran's foreign ministry confirmed it received proposals from international mediators aimed at easing hostilities, and a US official said talks are ongoing — the first reported contact since Tehran suspended its MoU commitments on July 18. Per §3.5.3 both statements are claims from interested parties: no mediator named, no venue, no agenda, and the previous two frameworks were announced with more specificity and collapsed anyway. The corroborating evidence is priced rather than spoken — Brent fell roughly $6 on a day that also delivered a tenth night of strikes and a formal second-chokepoint declaration, meaning traders weighted the diplomatic signal above both. Cutting against it, Trump's Truth Social pledge that Iran will pay 'many times over' for every American killed, transmitted to Hegseth, Caine and 'every Leader in the Military,' is a standing commitment that removes American discretion over the next escalation. The Houthi declaration compounds this: an Iran-aligned belligerent acting on its own grievance ledger can trigger the pledge without Tehran's involvement or consent.
