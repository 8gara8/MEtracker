Direction escalating; 7-day risk extreme; spillover critical. 30-day ceasefire probability eases to 6.

## Diplomatic state

Day 142 broke the top-level legal fiction and kept the working channel alive at the same time. Mojtaba Khamenei's written message — the US 'repeatedly breached its commitments,' the president's signature 'worthless and invalid,' Iran and the axis of resistance holding 'unforgettable lessons' — is the first supreme-leader-level repudiation of the MoU's validity, and it removes the authority any deal would need. Yet an Iranian delegation still flew to Oman to continue mediated talks, and Tehran barraged Qatar, the other mediator, on the same weekend. Repudiation, negotiation and bombardment are running on three parallel tracks, which is the command-authority gap operating in the open rather than a strategy. Inside Iran the pressure is structural: ultra-hardliners accuse Ghalibaf and Pezeshkian of plotting a 'coup,' and Araghchi remains the designated scapegoat. Great-power register unchanged: Beijing condemns the port blockade, Moscow holds its line. No ceasefire table has reconvened.
