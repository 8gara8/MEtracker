## Current war status

Day 109 hardens Day 108's declaration into a digital signature — but the binding text, the Hormuz toll language, and Israel's reserved right to strike all stay open. The US said President Trump, VP Vance and Iran's parliament speaker Mohammad Ghalibaf "digitally" signed the memorandum of understanding on Sunday (June 14), with the in-person signing still set for Friday, June 19, in Geneva, where Vance and Jared Kushner attend and Trump does not (Times of Israel, The Hill). Per §3.5.3 this is a firmer commitment than Day 108's mediator declaration, but Vance stayed vague on the deal's key elements and the binding text is released only after the ceremony (ABC News). The fault lines are open: Iran's Tasnim claimed the MoU leaves "management of maritime navigation services in the Strait of Hormuz" to Iran and Oman (a toll opening) while Trump insists Hormuz reopens immediately without tolls; Tehran spun the deal to its public as a "victory" even as Araghchi warned agreements can be "torn up" (Fox News, Tribune). On Lebanon the hottest Day 108 trigger cooled — Iran's "imminent" response to the Beirut strike did not come, reportedly forestalled by US pressure — but a senior US official said Israeli withdrawal is not a deal condition and Netanyahu vowed to "defend ourselves as necessary," with comprehensive Israel-Lebanon talks set for the week of June 22 (CNN, NPR). 30-day ceasefire probability rises to 55 from 50; direction stays de-escalating; spillover eases from critical to conditional.
