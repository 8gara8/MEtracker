## Current war status

Day 104 breaks the hundred-day standoff: the attack pause that held through Day 103 collapsed into direct US-Iran kinetic exchange on two consecutive nights. The sequence ran from a single incident — Iran downed a US Army Apache over the Strait of Hormuz on June 9 (both crew recovered unharmed by an uncrewed Navy boat), CENTCOM struck Iranian air-defense and radar sites in response, Iran salvoed missiles and drones at US bases in Bahrain (Fifth Fleet), Kuwait (Ali Al Salem), and Jordan (Azraq) early June 10 — reported nearly all intercepted with no US casualties against the IRGC's claim of 21 targets / 4 destroyed incl. an F-35 hangar — and the US struck Iran a second straight night, hitting air defense, drone command-and-control, ammunition depots, and warehouses across southern Iran (NBC, CBS, Al Jazeera, Axios). The strikes were pitched as calibrated, casualty-avoiding pressure — one option 'big in scale but short in duration' to move Tehran — even as Trump said the US would attack 'very hard' (CBS, NPR). The nuclear file hardened in parallel: the IAEA Board adopted the E3-drafted censure 21-3-10 (Russia, China, Niger opposed; 10 abstained), Iran/Russia/China issued a joint statement accusing Grossi of politicization and noting he recommended no punitive action, and Tehran moved to expand enrichment (IAEA, WANA, Arms Control Association). On the energy vector the IRGC declared the Strait of Hormuz closed to all vessels and Brent reversed Day 103's ease back above $96, EIA June STEO near $105 June-July — the Day 103 easing divergence resolved to the upside (Trading Economics, EIA). Against the escalation, Qatar sent a negotiating team to Tehran in coordination with Washington (joining Pakistan's channel) and Trump kept calling a deal possible while warning Netanyahu he would be 'on his own' if Israel escalated — the PM having called off a runway-ready strike (Business Standard, Times of Israel). 30-day ceasefire probability eases to 8. Direction holds escalating; seven-day risk raised to extreme.
